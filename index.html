URBANE MISFIT- ALARINKIRI

ALARINKIRI is a Yoruba word for TRAVELLER. I haven’t been called that a lot though but I hope to be someday. I love experiencing new things, seeing new places and taking pictures of landscapes.
Tourism is fast becoming a big deal in Nigeria, the country which is viewed as a multinational state, as it is inhabited by over 500 ethnic groups of which the three largest are the Hausa, Igbo and Yoruba; these ethnic groups speak over 500 different languages, diverse cultures, exhibiting unique art. The Nigerian people are blessed with a positive spirit and unique way of life that reflects across their Entertainment, Sports, Art & Culture industries. ‘Nollywood’, the local film industry is the third-largest in the world and the country maintains pioneering status with its music which has played major roles in influencing contemporary world music. A rich array of cuisines, fashion styles and festivals exist across the different tribes.
Some of the must-see places, you should definitely check out if you find yourself in Nigeria include;

1) Olumo Rock in Abeokuta, Ogun state.

2) Yankari Game Reserve, Bauchi

3) Lekki Conservation Center, Lagos state.

4) Agodi Gardens, Ibadan, Oyo state.

5) Kajuru Castle, Kaduna.

6) Nike-Art Gallery, Lagos state.

7) Obudu Cattle Ranch, Cross rivers.
